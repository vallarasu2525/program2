United States

Geography

The United States is the third-largest country in the world by land area, covering approximately 9.8 million square kilometers. It shares borders with Canada to the north and Mexico to the south.

Natural Wonders
The U.S. is home to breathtaking natural wonders, including the Grand Canyon, Yellowstone National Park, Niagara Falls, and the Great Lakes.
Culture
The U.S. has a diverse culture shaped by immigration, with influences from around the world. It is known for its contributions to music, film, technology, and fast food.
Government
The United States is a federal republic with a president as both head of state and head of government. It has a system of checks and balances across three branches: executive, legislative, and judicial.

History
The U.S. declared independence from Britain on July 4, 1776. It has played a major role in world history, including two world wars, the Cold War, and the advancement of civil rights.
